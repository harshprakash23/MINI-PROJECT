Maps of South Asian Countries

India

India is characterized by a vast landscape ranging from Himalayan peaks in the north to the Indian Ocean coastline in the south.

Pakistan

Pakistan shares borders with four countries and features a varied topography that includes deserts, forests, and mountain ranges.

Afghanistan

Located in the heart of Asia, Afghanistan has a rugged terrain dominated by mountains, contributing to its strategic importance and cultural evolution.

Bhutan

Bhutan, known for its monasteries and fortresses (or dzongs), is largely covered by mountainous terrain.

Bangladesh

Bangladesh is distinguished by its fertile plains and numerous rivers, contributing to its lush greenery and rich agricultural landscape.

Nepal

Nepal is home to eight of the world's ten tallest mountains, including Mount Everest, the highest point on Earth.

Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka is an island nation in the Indian Ocean, known for its diverse landscapes ranging from arid plains to highlands and sandy beaches.

Iran

Iran boasts a large plateau surrounded by mountains and is known for its historical cities and cultural heritage spanning thousands of years.

Maldives

The Maldives consists of 26 atolls stretching across the equator, renowned for its islands, coral reefs, and blue lagoons.
